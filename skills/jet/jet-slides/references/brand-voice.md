## Brand Purpose

**"Delivering moments of joy every day to everyone"**

Joy is JET's reason for existing. It's found in everything: the special, the everyday, the essential and the not-so-essential.

## Brand Platform: Did Somebody Say (DSS)

DSS captures the moment of inspiration customers feel before ordering. It's how JET makes sure that whatever they need and whenever they need it, they know JET is the solution.

**Key rules:**
- DSS is NEVER a question — it's always the answer
- Never add a question mark or punctuation to DSS
- The answer should always and only be the local JET brand name (Just Eat, Takeaway.com, Lieferando, etc.)

## Core Characteristics

The four characteristics that make up JET's brand voice:

### 1. Bold
*"We're confident in who we are and always deliver on our promises"*
- **Empowering** — inspires choice and positive action
- **Taking positive risks** — explore new angles, embrace new ideas
- **Looking to inspire** — flip expectations, open up new possibilities
- **Taking an original angle** — find a new way to interpret and own things

### 2. Relatable
*"We listen, we care and we understand"*
- **Appreciating our audience** — understand their perspective
- **Staying current** — stay in touch with what's happening in their world
- **Keeping it simple** — one key message, easy to understand, no jargon

### 3. Motivating
*"We help customers get what they want and what they need"*
- **Putting audience first** — look outwards, less "we" more "you"
- **Being clear on what we offer** — make it obvious what we're communicating
- **Driving action** — energy, movement, speed in language

### 4. Optimistic
*"We see and celebrate the joy in everything, however big or small"*
- **Staying upbeat** — does it bring joy? Could it bring a smile?
- **Making a connection** — share in things that make us happy
- **Surprising and delighting** — unexpected ways to make people smile (never cruel)

## Dos and Don'ts

### We Are
- Confident, Memorable, Surprising (Bold)
- Relevant, Emotive, Considered (Relatable)
- Purposeful, Clear, Simple (Motivating)
- Positive, Enthusiastic, Grounded (Optimistic)

### We Aren't
- Arrogant, Jarring, Shocking (Bold)
- Try-hard, Sentimental, Contrived (Relatable)
- Vague, Boring, Basic (Motivating)
- Cheesy, Intense, Unrealistic (Optimistic)

## Writing Style

### Conversational Tone
- Less "we", more "you" — make it about the audience
- Avoid jargon — use words the audience knows
- Grammar isn't everything — bend rules for conversational feel
- Use contractions — you're, we're, they're
- Change up sentence structure — alternate short and long
- Ask questions — create a true conversation
- Read copy aloud — if it doesn't sound natural, change it

### Active Voice
- Joy is an experience with energy: use active voice
- "We've had to cancel your order" (active) NOT "Your order has been cancelled" (passive)
- More verbs, fewer adjectives
- Go easy on adverbs
- Use short sentences
- When in doubt, put the subject first

### Writing for Translations
- Avoid local/regional references or slang
- Avoid ambiguity and wordplay
- Use "subject, verb, object" structure
- Consider cultural differences (food habits, religion, advertising conventions)
- Prioritise keeping things clear

## Using Humour

Our humour IS: Witty, Observant, Positive, Confident, Playful
Our humour is NOT: Try-hard, Surreal, Cruel, Arrogant, Rude

### Techniques
1. **Asides** — little quips in the middle of copy ("Sounds good, doesn't it?")
2. **Sign-offs** — little quips at the end ("You're welcome.", "Don't forget to save room for dessert…")
3. **Double-meaning** — witty wordplay ("Get it before it's scone", "Taco 'bout a bargain")
4. **Rhyming** — adds rhythm ("Plant-based food for every mood")
5. **Relevant references** — cultural, seasonal, audience-specific ("Rain ruined your BBQ plans? We've got it sorted.")
6. **Exaggeration** — bold but knowing ("That's delivery without the fees. Zero. Zilch. Nada. Nothing.")
7. **Emojis** — in CRM and social only; use default yellow skin tone; keep it clean

**Avoid jokes about:** race, gender, politics, religion, or controversial topics. Never joke about couriers or customers.

## Typography for Tone

- **Lowercase headlines** — for informal/consumer-facing (B2C) comms, creates informality
- **Sentence case** — for formal/corporate comms
- Always capitalise proper nouns (brand names, product titles)
- Some markets capitalise all nouns (e.g., German)

## Language to Avoid

### Brand Terms
- **Cheap** / Great value — devalues the brand
- **Fast food** — JET is about empowering everyday convenience
- **Takeaway** / Takeout — JET provides wider variety beyond local takeaways

### Food Terms
- Authentic / Traditional — can't presume origin of a dish
- Booze / references to being "drunk" — cheapens brand
- Grub — unpleasant term for food
- Hangover cure — cheapens brand image

### Emotional Terms
- **Hungry / Starving / Famished** — downplays real hunger
- **Lazy / Laze** — negative emotional state
- **Naughty** — negative emotional state
- **Guilty** (secret) — portrays offerings negatively
- **Greedy** — don't promote overconsumption
- **Diet** — not inclusive, can't assume eating habits
- Drool / Dribbling — unpleasant
- **Swearing** (direct or indirect, e.g. "Clucking good chicken") — not in line with brand purpose

| Partners | Business owners (restaurants, grocery stores, etc.) | Cost and efficiency |
| Couriers | Delivery personnel (JET pays them) | Hire and retain talent |
| Employees | Internal staff | Belonging, identity, vision |

| Corporate customers | Offer JET Pay to their employees | Flexible solutions |
